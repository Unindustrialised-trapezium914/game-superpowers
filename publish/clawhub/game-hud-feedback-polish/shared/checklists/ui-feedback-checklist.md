# UI / Feedback Checklist

## Information hierarchy
- Is the most important information visually dominant?
- Can the player see health, objective, score, ammo, timer, or equivalent without scanning?
- Are secondary details visually quieter?

## Readability
- Sufficient contrast
- Adequate spacing
- Labels short and unambiguous
- Text large enough for the target device

## Affordance
- Buttons look tappable/clickable
- Hover/focus/pressed states are distinct when relevant
- Invalid actions provide feedback

## Motion / response
- Motion supports meaning
- Success and failure states feel distinct
- Transitions are short and do not interrupt flow
- Input is acknowledged fast enough to feel intentional

## Feel / juice
- Hit confirmation
- Damage / danger feedback
- Reward feedback
- UI reactions for important events
- Audio pairing for important interactions

## Mobile thumb-zone sanity
- Critical actions reachable
- Avoid cramped corners for primary controls unless justified
- Important HUD not hidden by fingers

## Clutter
- Remove decorative noise that hurts play
- Preserve empty space around critical elements
